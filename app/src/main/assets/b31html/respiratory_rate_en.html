Respiration rate     

Medical examination explanation

    During human sleep, the normal breathing rate is 18-26 times per minute, and the average value and the highest value exceed 18-26: it can be determined that the breathing rate is too high. If it exceeds a certain range, breathing difficulties will occur. Recently hospital rescue.

Typical conditions

    (1) Bradycardia: The breathing rhythm is neat but the frequency is less than 10 times per minute, which is common at the end of life or before apnea. Bradycardia can also occur due to inhibition of the respiratory center when neurological, metabolic diseases, or overdose of certain drugs.

(2) Shortness of breath: shortness of breath and feeling of breathing distress.

Treatment methods

For breathing difficulties, the emergency measures at home are as follows:

1. Keep quiet and avoid the emotional stress of the patient to prevent aggravating breathing difficulties.

2. Take a semi-recumbent or sitting position to reduce fatigue and oxygen consumption.

3. Keep indoor air fresh and well ventilated.

4. Give a light diet and encourage patients to eat more vegetables and fruits to supplement their body water.

5. Appropriately administer phlegm and antispasmodic drugs such as bipentin and aminophylline to keep the airway open.

6. When the condition is critical, send to nearby hospital for rescue in time.
